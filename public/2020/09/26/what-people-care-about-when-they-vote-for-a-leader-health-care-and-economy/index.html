What people care about when they vote for a leader? Health Care and Economy!

2020-09-26

Introduction

In this report, I investigated the 2008 CES dataset (Gidengil, E, Everitt, J, Fournier, P and Nevitte, N. 2009), which is short for Canadian Election Study. The CES consists a survey that was conducted during the second half of the election compaign and there were about 4500 eligible voters participated in the survey (Gidengil, E, Everitt, J, Fournier, P and Nevitte, N. 2009). This dataset effectively reflects the politics of scale of major Canadians, and how they generally expect from the country leader.

There were three surveys completed: Campaign Period Survey, Post Election Survey, and Mailback Survey. As their names suggested, these surveys were taken at different times around the time of 2008 Canadian Election. All surveys were completed by respondents via web, mail or telephone.I chose to  focus on the data provided by the campaign period survey (the CPS) due to a large number of variables and observations. An interesting finding of my investigation on this particular subset of data was, a majority of respondents seems to value the environment a lot, which shows that environment protection could be an influential factor in how they vote during election. In fact, when the respondents were asked “What is the most important issue to YOU PERSONALLY in this FEDERAL election? ”, about 10 percent of them chose “environmental/ecological issue”.

This report have several weakness that need to be paid attention in future study. First, only 45 variables were chosen to analyze while there is a total of 541 variables in the dataset. This means that the statistical summaries in this report is not thorough enough and the final finding might not be accurate; Second, in the original survey dataset, there are about 42% of the observations missing, which means respondents did not response to some particular questions. This could result in response bias, which might result in inaccurate findings as well. This problem could be avoided if there are fewer questions included in the survey, because I think people’s interests would likely wore down while they are taking the survey with too many questions.
